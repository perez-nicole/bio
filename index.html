About me.

Curiosity drives me.

From 2014-2015 I lived and taught English in the Republic of Georgia (Sakartvelo), and during my time there, I studied the Georgian language, explored ancient Roman ruins, and made treks to Israel, Armenia and eastern Europe. The travels ignited in me an enduring fascination with the history and culture of the ancient Near East, and the interplay of anthropology, psychology and science with history.



Professionally, I’m a data science industry vet, having worked as a data scientist across domains as diverse as health and life sciences (UnitedHealth Group, Mercy), psychology and marketing (General Mills), and transportation/urban planning (Metropolitan Council). I’ve authored numerous internal white papers de-complicating analytical findings for non-technical audiences.



I hold a master’s of arts in Social Sciences from the University of Chicago and a master’s of science in Data Science from the University of Minnesota. At the University of Minnesota, I was also the recipient of a 2021-22 NSF NRT fellowship to conduct research on the detection of gravitational waves using modern machine learning techniques.
